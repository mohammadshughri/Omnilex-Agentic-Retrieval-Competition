End-to-End: val_001

"…three-month extension of pre-trial detention under Art. 221 Abs. 1 lit. b StPO (risk of collusion)…" (English val query)
✕ BM25 Path
Step 1 — Token lookup
Scans body text for: "detention", "collusion", "pre-trial"
Step 2 — Match attempt
German corpus contains: "Haft", "Kollusion", "Untersuchungshaft" — zero token overlap.
Step 3 — Art. 221 StPO?
Article numbers are in citation metadata, not the body text BM25 indexes. Retrieves unrelated articles by matching incidental tokens — numbers, abbreviations — not legal meaning.
F1 = 0.00 — returns irrelevant matches
✓ BGE-M3 Path (Measured Overall)
Step 1 — Anchor extraction
Regex finds "Art. 221 StPO" → injected as priority candidate before embedding.
Step 2 — Dense embedding
BGE-M3 turns the query into a meaning-vector. This lets English "detention" land near German "Haft" and related law passages.
Step 3 — Combine candidates
What we hope dense retrieval adds: Art. 227, Art. 212 (semantically related detention articles). Overall laws-only run now measures above zero.
Measured overall: Macro F1 = 0.0435 at top-10
Takeaway Dense retrieval bridges the language gap. Anchors add precision for queries with explicit article references. Together they cover both exact and conceptual matches.
